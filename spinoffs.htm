‘Tommy Minecraft’

(2021 Spin-off show)

A one-series spin-off of ‘Tommy’ that involves Tommy and Geo being transported into a Minecraft after a school trip mishap. They have to try to get out by beating the game, but will enemies block the way?

 

This show was actually meant to be S2E3, as S2’s answer to ‘Kidnapped’ (S1E4) having a “special” live action clip, but a technical issue meant it had to be uploaded as its own show.

‘Road To Ninja’

(2025-Present Spin-off show)

A spin-off about the show’s two main ninjas and teachers, Christopher ‘Long Potato Bauble Head’ Jones and Assassin Boldheart, diving deep into the duo’s history, education mishaps and more.

‘Catching Michael!’

(2022 Promotional game)

A game made to promote ‘Tommy’ where the objective is to click on fast-moving protagonists and avoid the slow antagonists.

 

It’s actually a reskin of ‘Catching Daniel The Game’ (2020), which was also developed by the ‘Software & Gaming’ division of Geo Productions for YouTuber ‘ENSTREEM’, though that game has now been delisted.

 

It has received a few updates and there was also a planned re-texture of the game based on the show’s new higher-quality recording methods, but to this day it hasn’t been released.

‘Tommy World!’

(2023 Promotional Minecraft Map)

Created to build hype for the upcoming fourth series of ‘Tommy’, ‘Tommy World!’ is a digital ‘Minecraft’ themepark featuring various rides and attractions. It has received some updates over time.

’Tommy Group’

(2021 Google Classroom - Discontinued; Forum on website - discontinued; 2026 PowerPost Group)

The ‘Tommy Fan Club’ was created as a place for dedicated fans of the show to hang out - it was replaced by the ‘Community’ tab (formerly ‘Forum’) on the Tommy Site, but that in itself was replaced by a ‘Tommy’ group on social media platform PowerPost.

‘Tommy Site’

(2022-Present Website)

The place to be for the latest on ‘Tommy’ and More! It includes a blog as well as easy links to stream ‘Tommy’ and its spinoffs.

‘TommyPedia’ & ‘Tommy Wiki’

(2023-2025 Wiki websites)

In 2023, Geo Productions released ‘TommyPedia’, designed to be a free encyclopedia of all things ‘Tommy’. It was eventually discontinued for a new ‘Tommy Wiki’ That you’re reading right now!

Tommy Channel (Pop-up TV channel)

Tommy Channel has been a pop-up live TV channel, not available on traditional over-the-air formats but delivered via YouTube’s live streaming function. It showed ‘Tommy’ episodes and if it returns in the future it may also show other ‘Tommy’ related content. You can still watch recordings of it online. There was going to be a chart show showing all ‘Tommy’ episodes from least to most popular, but it was cancelled.
